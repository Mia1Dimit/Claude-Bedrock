# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training Athlete

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete training for a marathon with strategic carbohydrate timing around a 20km morning run:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (110g), Fat (80g), Carbs (440g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Pre-Run Breakfast (6:30 AM - 90 minutes before run):** White bread toast (80g) with jam (40g) and banana (120g). Orange juice (250ml).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 3g, Carbs: 95g

* **During Run (10:00 AM):** Sports drink (500ml) containing glucose and electrolytes, consumed in 2-3 portions during the 20km run.
    * Protein: 0g, Fat: 0g, Carbs: 60g

* **Post-Run Recovery Snack (11:15 AM - within 30 minutes):** White bagel (100g) with honey (30g) and Greek yogurt (150g).
    * Protein: 20g, Fat: 4g, Carbs: 70g

* **Lunch (1:30 PM):** Lentil and vegetable soup (400ml), whole wheat pita bread (120g), hummus (40g), and fresh strawberries (150g).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 85g

* **Afternoon Snack (4:00 PM):** Rice cakes (50g) with tahini (25g) and sliced banana (100g).
    * Protein: 9g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 65g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Chickpea pasta (120g dry weight) with marinara sauce (200g), sautéed spinach (150g), and olive oil (15ml).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 20g, Carbs: 85g

* **Evening Snack (9:00 PM):** Rice pudding made with milk (200g) and honey (20g).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 6g, Carbs: 35g

This meal plan strategically front-loads carbohydrates before and immediately after the 20km training run to maximize glycogen replenishment and fuel endurance performance, while meeting all vegetarian and nut-free requirements.
